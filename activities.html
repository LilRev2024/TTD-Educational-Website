Regional Science Project 2021

Okay, this is the hardest part of the whole project…picking your topic. But here are some ideas to get you started. Even if you don’t like any, they may inspire you to come up with one of your own. Remember, check all project ideas with your teacher and parents, and don’t do any project that would hurt or scare people or animals. Good luck!

Project topics:

  1. Can the food we eat affect our heart rate?
  2. Can background noise levels affect how well we concentrate?
  3. What is the best way to keep cut flowers fresh the longest?
  4. What light brightness makes plants grow the best?
  5. Does the color of a room affect human behavior?

Recreational Activities

The main goal of recreational activities is to help children learn about other types of skills other than their academic skills. Each student will participate in two or more recreational activities for the term.The activities may include but lot limited to:

  1. Audit your social media accounts.
  2. Create one good habit and stick with it.
  3. Develop a list of jobs that sound interesting.
  4. Look for a summer internship.
  5. Shadow someone at their job during spring break.
  6. Visit a college or two.
  7. Try one thing that scares (or challenges) your teen like eating sushi, karaoke, or public speaking.
  8. Volunteer in a community effort for your local food pantry or other charity.
  9. Help plan a spring dance or party that benefits a charity.
  10. Make cookies for a neighbor.
